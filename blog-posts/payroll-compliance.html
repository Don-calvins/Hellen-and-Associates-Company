Introduction

Payroll compliance is one of the most critical responsibilities for any employer in Kenya. With multiple statutory deductions, strict deadlines, and severe penalties for non-compliance, getting payroll right is essential for business success and legal operation.

This comprehensive checklist will guide you through every aspect of payroll compliance, from initial employer registration to monthly filings and annual returns. Whether you're a new employer or looking to improve your current processes, this guide has you covered.

📌 Key Statistic

According to KRA, over 60% of payroll penalties result from missed deadlines or incorrect calculations. Proper compliance can save your business millions in penalties.

Step 1: Employer Registration Checklist

Before processing payroll, ensure you're properly registered with all relevant authorities:

✓

KRA PIN Registration

Ensure your business has a valid KRA PIN and is registered for PAYE

✓

NSSF Registration

Register as an employer with NSSF and obtain employer registration number

✓

SHIF Registration

Register for Social Health Insurance Fund (replacing NHIF)

✓

Housing Levy Registration

Register for the Affordable Housing Levy (1.5% of gross salary)

✓

NITA Registration

Register with the National Industrial Training Authority (if applicable)

Step 2: Payroll Setup Checklist

Employee Documentation

  • Collect KRA PIN certificates from all employees
  • Obtain ID copies and next of kin details
  • Sign employment contracts as required by law
  • Set up employee files with all statutory forms
  • Collect bank details for salary payments

Payroll System Setup

  • Choose a compliant payroll system or software
  • Configure tax bands and reliefs correctly
  • Set up statutory deduction rates
  • Create payslip templates with all required details
  • Set up payment schedules and reminders

💡 Pro Tip

Use a digital payroll system that automatically updates statutory rates. This prevents errors when rates change and ensures you're always compliant.

Step 3: Monthly Compliance Checklist

Before Payroll Processing

  • Verify employee attendance and leave records
  • Check for new hires or terminations
  • Update any salary changes or promotions
  • Confirm statutory deduction changes (NSSF tiers, SHIF rates)

During Payroll Processing

  • Calculate gross pay (basic + allowances + commissions)
  • Apply statutory deductions correctly
  • Calculate net pay
  • Generate payslips for all employees
  • Maintain payroll register

After Payroll (Before 9th of each month)

  • Process salary payments to employees
  • Prepare statutory payment schedules
  • Generate P10 (PAYE return)
  • File monthly returns with KRA
  • Remit all statutory deductions

⚠️ Late Payment Penalties

  • PAYE Late Payment: 5% of tax due plus 1% per month interest
  • NSSF Late Payment: KES 10,000 or imprisonment
  • SHIF Late Payment: 2% of contribution per month
  • Housing Levy: 5% penalty plus 1% interest per month

Annual Filing Requirements

By 31st January Each Year:

  • Issue P9 forms to all employees (summary of annual earnings and deductions)
  • File annual PAYE return (P9A/P9B) with KRA
  • Submit NSSF annual return with schedule of contributions
  • File SHIF annual return
  • Prepare and submit Housing Levy annual return

Additional Annual Requirements:

  • Reconcile all statutory payments with annual returns
  • Update employee records and contracts
  • Review payroll processes and internal controls
  • Prepare for potential audits by statutory authorities

Common Payroll Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Incorrect Employee Classification

Misclassifying employees as independent contractors to avoid statutory deductions is illegal and carries severe penalties.

❌ Missing Deadlines

The 9th of each month is a hard deadline. Even one day late triggers penalties and interest.

❌ Wrong Tax Calculations

Using incorrect tax bands, forgetting personal relief, or miscalculating pro-rated salaries for new hires.

❌ Poor Record Keeping

Failure to maintain proper payroll records for the required 5-year period can result in audit issues.

❌ Ignoring Rate Changes

Statutory rates change periodically. Using outdated rates leads to underpayment and penalties.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

KRA Penalties

  • Late filing of PAYE return: KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due (whichever higher)
  • Late payment of PAYE: 5% of tax due + 1% interest per month
  • Underpayment of tax: 20% of the tax underpaid
  • Failure to issue P9 forms: KES 100,000

NSSF Penalties

  • Late payment: KES 10,000 or imprisonment for up to 6 months
  • Failure to register: KES 100,000 or imprisonment
  • Non-remittance: Up to KES 500,000 or 3 years imprisonment

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens if I pay salary late?

A: Late salary payments don't affect statutory deadlines. You must still remit deductions by the 9th, even if employees haven't been paid.

Q: How do I handle payroll for part-time employees?

A: Part-time employees are subject to the same statutory deductions as full-time employees, calculated on actual earnings.

Q: What records must I keep and for how long?

A: Keep all payroll records, including attendance, calculations, and payment proof for at least 5 years.

Q: Can I use Excel for payroll?

A: While possible, manual Excel payroll is error-prone. We recommend compliant payroll software for accuracy and audit trails.

Q: What if an employee doesn't have a KRA PIN?

A: Employees must have KRA PINs before employment. You cannot process payroll without it.
