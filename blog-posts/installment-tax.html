Introduction

Many businesses in Kenya focus solely on their annual tax return and forget about installment tax payments throughout the year. This oversight can lead to significant penalties and interest charges that could have been easily avoided.

Installment tax requires businesses to pay their estimated tax liability in four equal installments during the year, rather than a single lump sum at the end. This guide explains everything you need to know about installment tax, including who must pay, when to pay, how to calculate it, and what happens if you miss a payment.

📌 Key Fact

Installment tax applies to companies and individuals with business income. Failure to pay on time results in a 20% penalty plus 1% interest per month.

What is Installment Tax?

Installment tax is a system of paying income tax in four equal installments throughout the year, based on the estimated tax for that year. It applies to:

  • Companies: Must pay corporate tax in installments
  • Individuals: With business or professional income
  • Partnerships: Partners pay based on their share of income

How It Works

If your estimated tax for the year is KES 1,000,000, you pay:

  • 1st Installment: KES 250,000 by 30th April
  • 2nd Installment: KES 250,000 by 30th September
  • 3rd Installment: KES 250,000 by 31st December
  • 4th Installment: KES 250,000 by 31st March

Who Must Pay Installment Tax?

✅ Required to Pay

  • All registered companies
  • Individuals with business income > KES 500,000
  • Partnerships
  • Professional practices
  • Landlords with rental income

❌ Exempted

  • Employees (PAYE only)
  • Turnover Tax (ToT) payers
  • Presumptive Tax payers
  • First year of business

First Year Exception

New businesses are exempt from installment tax in their first year of operation. They pay the full tax when filing their first return.

⚠️ Important Note

For companies with different accounting periods, installments are due on the 30th day of the 4th, 6th, 9th, and 12th months after the start of their accounting period.

How to Calculate Installment Tax

Installment tax is based on the estimated tax for the current year. The estimate should be based on:

  • Prior Year's Tax: Use last year's tax as a baseline (most common method)
  • Projected Income: If you expect significant changes, use projected figures
  • Latest Available Information: Use the most recent financial data

📝 Example Calculation

Previous Year's Tax: KES 1,200,000
Estimated Current Year Tax: KES 1,200,000 (using prior year)
Each Installment: KES 1,200,000 ÷ 4 = KES 300,000

📝 Example with Growth

Previous Year's Tax: KES 1,200,000
Expected Growth: 20%
Estimated Tax: KES 1,200,000 × 1.2 = KES 1,440,000
Each Installment: KES 1,440,000 ÷ 4 = KES 360,000

Estimating Your Tax

Methods of Estimation

Method Description Best For
Prior Year Basis Use last year's tax liability Stable businesses
Projected Basis Estimate based on current year projections Growing or declining businesses
Nil Estimation Estimate zero if expecting a loss Businesses expecting losses

Important Rules

  • If you underestimate, you'll pay the balance with your annual return (plus interest)
  • If you overestimate, you can claim a refund or offset against future tax
  • Reasonable estimates are accepted; deliberate underpayment attracts penalties

Payment Process

How to Pay Installment Tax

1. Login to iTax

Go to itax.kra.go.ke and login with your credentials.

2. Select Payment Type

Navigate to "Payments" → "Create Payment Registration" → Select "Installment Tax"

3. Enter Amount

Enter the installment amount for the period.

4. Generate Payment Slip

Generate and print the payment slip (PRN).

5. Make Payment

Pay via M-Pesa, bank, or PesaLink using the PRN.

💡 Payment Methods

  • M-Pesa: Paybill 572572, Account: PRN number
  • Bank: KRA Collection Accounts at all commercial banks
  • PesaLink: Send to KRA via participating banks

Penalties for Late Payment

Offense Penalty
Late payment of installment 20% of the unpaid tax
Late payment interest 1% per month on unpaid amount
Failure to pay any installment 20% penalty + 1% monthly interest
Underpayment > 20% of actual 20% of the underpayment

📝 Penalty Example

Installment Due: KES 250,000
Paid 2 months late:
- Late Payment Penalty: KES 250,000 × 20% = KES 50,000
- Interest: KES 250,000 × 1% × 2 = KES 5,000
Total Penalty: KES 55,000

⚠️ Automatic Penalties

KRA automatically calculates and applies penalties for late payment. These appear in your iTax ledger and must be paid before you can file future returns.

Revisions & Adjustments

Can I revise my estimates?

Yes, you can revise your installment tax estimates if your circumstances change:

  • Increase estimate: Pay the higher amount in remaining installments
  • Decrease estimate: Reduce remaining installments (with justification)
  • Loss situation: File nil installments with explanation

Revision Rules

  • Revisions must be reasonable and justifiable
  • KRA may request supporting documents
  • Frequent or dramatic revisions may trigger audit
  • You can't revise downward after the due date of an installment

What if I paid too much?

If your total installments exceed your actual tax liability:

  • The excess will be refunded after filing your annual return
  • You can request offset against future tax liabilities
  • Refunds typically take 30-90 days to process

Special Rules for New Businesses

🆕

First Year of Business

New businesses are exempt from installment tax in their first year of operation.

What to do: Pay the full tax when filing your first return by 30th June of the following year.

📊

Second Year Onwards

Starting from your second year, you must pay installment tax based on the previous year's tax.

Estimate: Use your first year's tax as the baseline for your second year installments.

💡 New Business Tip

Even though exempt in your first year, consider making voluntary installment payments to manage cash flow. This helps avoid a large lump sum payment when filing your first return.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Forgetting Installment Dates

Many businesses remember only the annual filing deadline (30th June) and forget the installment dates.

❌ Using Wrong Estimate

Using outdated or incorrect figures for estimation, leading to underpayment penalties.

❌ Not Adjusting for Growth

Failing to increase installments when business is growing significantly.

❌ Late Payment

Paying even one day late triggers the 20% penalty plus interest.

❌ Ignoring Penalty Notices

Not addressing penalty notices promptly, allowing interest to accumulate.

❌ Incorrect PRN Usage

Using the wrong PRN or payment method, causing payment not to reflect in iTax.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I miss an installment payment?

▼

Pay immediately with penalty. The penalty is 20% of the unpaid amount plus 1% interest per month. Delaying only increases the amount owed.

Can I pay more than the required installment?

▼

Yes, you can pay more. The excess will be credited to your tax account and offset against future installments or refunded.

What if my business makes a loss?

▼

You can file nil installments. However, you must have reasonable justification. If you later make a profit, you'll need to pay the tax when filing your return.

Do I need to file a return for installment tax?

▼

No separate return is required. Installments are just payments. You still file your annual return by 30th June.

Can I use last year's tax if I expect lower profits?

▼

Yes, but if your actual tax is significantly higher, you may face underpayment penalties. It's safer to estimate based on realistic projections.

How do I know if I've underpaid?

▼

When you file your annual return, iTax will calculate the balance due. If your installments were less than 80% of the actual tax, you may incur penalties.
