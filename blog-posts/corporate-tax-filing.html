Introduction

Corporate tax filing is one of the most important compliance obligations for companies operating in Kenya. With strict deadlines, complex calculations, and severe penalties for non-compliance, understanding the corporate tax process is essential for every business owner and finance professional.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know about corporate tax filing in Kenya, from understanding who must file to step-by-step instructions for completing your returns accurately and on time.

📌 Key Fact

Corporate tax contributes approximately 30% of KRA's total revenue collection. In 2023, KRA collected over KES 2.1 trillion in total revenue, with corporate tax being a significant portion.

Who Must File Corporate Tax?

The following entities are required to file corporate tax returns in Kenya:

  • Limited Liability Companies - Both private and public companies registered in Kenya
  • Branches of Foreign Companies - Operating in Kenya
  • Partnerships - Registered partnerships (filed under individual partners or as a partnership)
  • Trusts - Including family trusts and charitable trusts (with exemptions)
  • Cooperative Societies - Registered cooperatives

💡 Exemptions

Some entities are exempt from corporate tax, including:

  • Charitable organizations with valid exemption certificates
  • Certain religious organizations
  • Diplomatic missions
  • Specific entities exempted by law (e.g., some export processing zones)

Corporate Tax Rates in Kenya

Resident Companies

30%

Standard rate for all resident companies

Non-Resident Companies

37.5%

Higher rate for branches of foreign companies

SMEs (Turnover Tax)

3%

For businesses with turnover between KES 1M - 50M

Export Processing Zones

10% (first 10 yrs)

Reduced rate for EPZ companies

Special Rates for Specific Sectors

  • Manufacturing: 30% (standard) but with various investment deductions
  • Agriculture: 30% with enhanced deductions for farm works
  • Mining: Varies based on agreement, typically 30-37.5%
  • Insurance: 30% plus branch tax for reinsurance

Filing Deadlines & Timeline

By 30th April

Installment Tax Payment 1

First quarterly installment of estimated tax for the year (based on previous year's tax)

By 30th June

Annual Return Filing Deadline

File annual corporate tax return for the previous financial year

By 30th Sept

Installment Tax Payment 2

Second quarterly installment (total 50% of estimated tax)

By 31st Dec

Installment Tax Payment 3

Third quarterly installment (total 75% of estimated tax)

By 31st March

Installment Tax Payment 4

Final quarterly installment (100% of estimated tax)

⚠️ Important Note

The financial year for most companies ends on 31st December. However, companies can choose different year-ends with KRA approval. The filing deadline is always the 6th month after year-end.

Step-by-Step Filing Process on iTax

1

Login to iTax

Access itax.kra.go.ke and login with your company KRA PIN and password. Ensure you have administrator rights for tax filing.

2

Select Return Type

Navigate to "Returns" menu and select "File Return". Choose "Income Tax Return for Companies (Form C)" from the dropdown.

3

Select Period

Choose the tax period (financial year) you're filing for. The system will show available periods based on your registration.

4

Enter Financial Information

Complete the online form with:

  • Turnover/Gross income
  • Cost of sales
  • Operating expenses
  • Other income
  • Capital allowances
  • Losses brought forward
5

Review Calculations

The system automatically calculates tax payable based on your entries. Review all figures carefully.

6

Attach Financial Statements

Upload your audited or certified financial statements in PDF format (max 5MB).

7

Submit Return

Declare that the information is true and correct, then submit. You'll receive an acknowledgment receipt.

8

Pay Any Tax Due

If tax is payable, generate a payment slip and pay via M-Pesa, bank, or PesaLink before the deadline.

Installment Tax Payments

Companies must pay tax in installments throughout the year based on the previous year's tax liability.

Installment Due Date Percentage
1st Installment 30th April 25%
2nd Installment 30th September 25% (50% cumulative)
3rd Installment 31st December 25% (75% cumulative)
4th Installment 31st March 25% (100% cumulative)

How Installment Tax Works

If your previous year's tax was KES 1,000,000, you should pay KES 250,000 by each installment date. If actual tax is higher, you pay the balance when filing returns. If lower, you get a refund or credit.

Allowable Deductions

The following expenses are generally allowable deductions when calculating taxable income:

📦

Cost of Sales

Direct materials, labor, and overheads

🏢

Rent & Utilities

Business premises rent, electricity, water

👥

Salaries & Wages

Employee compensation (with PAYE paid)

📞

Operating Expenses

Office supplies, communication, travel

💰

Interest Expense

On business loans (subject to thin cap rules)

📉

Depreciation

Capital allowances at prescribed rates

Non-Allowable Deductions

  • Personal expenses
  • Capital expenditure (except through capital allowances)
  • Fines and penalties
  • Donations (unless to approved charities)
  • Interest on capital
  • Tax paid (corporate tax, VAT, etc.)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Missing Installment Tax Deadlines

Many companies focus only on the annual return and forget quarterly installments, leading to penalties.

❌ Incorrect Expense Classification

Claiming non-allowable expenses or misclassifying capital vs. revenue expenditure.

❌ Not Reconciling with Financial Statements

The iTax return figures must match your audited financial statements. Discrepancies trigger audits.

❌ Forgetting Loss Carry Forward

Tax losses can be carried forward for up to 9 years (5 years for some sectors).

❌ Incorrect Capital Allowances

Using wrong rates or forgetting to claim investment deductions on new assets.

❌ Related Party Transactions

Not applying arm's length pricing for transactions with related companies.

Penalties for Late Filing or Non-Compliance

Offense Penalty
Late filing of return KES 10,000 or 5% of tax due (whichever higher)
Late payment of tax 5% of tax due + 1% interest per month
Underpayment of tax 20% of the underpaid amount
Failure to keep proper records KES 100,000
False or misleading information 200% of tax evaded or imprisonment
Failure to register for tax KES 50,000

⚠️ Criminal Prosecution

Tax evasion is a criminal offense in Kenya. Conviction can lead to imprisonment for up to 5 years and/or a fine of up to KES 5,000,000.

Preparing for a Tax Audit

KRA may select your company for a tax audit. Here's how to prepare:

Step 1

Maintain Organized Records

Keep all financial records, invoices, receipts, and contracts for at least 5 years.

Step 2

Reconcile Regularly

Monthly reconciliation of VAT, PAYE, and corporate tax figures with financial statements.

Step 3

Document Related Party Transactions

Maintain transfer pricing documentation if you transact with related companies.

Step 4

Respond Promptly

If KRA requests information, respond within the given timeframe (usually 14 days).

Step 5

Engage a Tax Professional

Have a tax expert represent you during the audit process.

Special Rules for SMEs

Turnover Tax (ToT)

Small businesses with annual turnover between KES 1 million and KES 50 million can opt for Turnover Tax at 3% of gross turnover, instead of corporate tax.

Presumptive Tax

Businesses with turnover below KES 1 million pay a presumptive tax of KES 15,000 per year (for specified trades).

Which is better for your SME?

If your profit margin is above 10%, Turnover Tax (3% of turnover) may be higher than corporate tax (30% of profit). Calculate both to choose the optimal regime.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When is the deadline for filing corporate tax returns?

A: For companies with a December year-end, the deadline is 30th June. For other year-ends, it's the 6th month after year-end.

Q: What if my company made a loss?

A: You must still file a return. Losses can be carried forward to offset future profits for up to 9 years.

Q: Can I file corporate tax returns online?

A: Yes, all corporate tax returns must be filed through the iTax portal. Paper returns are no longer accepted.

Q: Do I need audited accounts to file corporate tax?

A: Yes, companies must attach audited financial statements. SMEs may attach certified accounts from a CPA.

Q: What is the penalty for late filing?

A: The greater of KES 10,000 or 5% of the tax due, plus interest at 1% per month on unpaid tax.

Q: Can I amend a filed return?

A: Yes, you can file an amended return within 5 years, but penalties may apply if additional tax is due.
