Introduction

Value Added Tax (VAT) is one of the most complex and frequently audited taxes in Kenya. With a standard rate of 16%, multiple zero-rated and exempt supplies, and strict invoicing requirements, VAT compliance can be challenging for businesses.

According to KRA, VAT-related errors account for over 40% of all tax audits, and penalties can quickly accumulate into millions of shillings. This guide will help you understand VAT requirements and avoid common mistakes that trigger audits and penalties.

📌 Key Fact

VAT contributes approximately 30% of KRA's total tax revenue. In 2023, KRA collected over KES 600 billion in VAT.

VAT Basics

VAT is a consumption tax levied on the supply of taxable goods and services in Kenya. It works on a credit mechanism:

📤

Output VAT

VAT charged on your sales to customers

16% of sales
📥

Input VAT

VAT paid on your business purchases

Claimable on expenses

VAT Payable = Output VAT - Input VAT

If Input VAT > Output VAT, you may claim a refund

VAT Registration Thresholds

Mandatory Registration

KES 5M

Annual turnover exceeding KES 5 million

Must register

Voluntary Registration

Below KES 5M

Businesses can opt to register voluntarily

Optional

⚠️ Failure to Register

If your turnover exceeds KES 5 million and you fail to register for VAT, you risk penalties of up to KES 100,000 plus back taxes from the date registration was required.

💡 Critical Distinction

Zero-rated vs Exempt: Zero-rated supplies allow you to claim input VAT on related expenses. Exempt supplies do NOT allow input VAT recovery. This is one of the most common sources of errors.

Top 10 VAT Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

❌

Mistake #1: Claiming Input VAT on Exempt Supplies

Penalty: 20% of wrongly claimed VAT

Claiming input VAT on expenses related to exempt supplies is illegal and a major audit trigger.

✅ Solution:

Maintain separate records for exempt and taxable supplies. Use partial exemption calculations if you make both types of supplies.

❌

Mistake #2: Missing the 20th of the Month Deadline

Penalty: 5% of tax due + 2% interest per month

VAT returns are due on the 20th of each month for the previous month's transactions.

✅ Solution:

Set up calendar reminders. File early to avoid system downtime issues on deadline day.

❌

Mistake #3: Incorrect VAT Treatment of Exports

Penalty: 20% of tax underpaid

Exports should be zero-rated, not exempt. Failing to provide proof of export can lead to disallowed zero-rating.

✅ Solution:

Keep proper export documentation: customs entries, bill of lading, proof of payment from foreign customer.

❌

Mistake #4: Invalid Tax Invoices

Penalty: KES 20,000 per invalid invoice

Claiming input VAT on invoices that don't meet KRA requirements (missing PIN, serial number, etc.).

✅ Solution:

Verify all supplier invoices have: KRA PIN, serial number, date, description, VAT amount, and total.

❌

Mistake #5: Not Reconciling with eTIMS

Penalty: KES 10,000 per invoice mismatch

Your VAT return must match eTIMS sales data. Discrepancies trigger automatic system flags.

✅ Solution:

Reconcile eTIMS sales reports with your VAT return before filing. Investigate any variances.

❌

Mistake #6: Claiming VAT on Restricted Items

Penalty: 20% of claimed amount

Certain expenses have restricted input VAT recovery: entertainment, passenger vehicles, etc.

✅ Solution:

Know the restrictions: Entertainment (50% recoverable), vehicles (limited), club fees (0%).

❌

Mistake #7: Late Invoicing

Penalty: KES 10,000 per late invoice

eTIMS requires invoices to be generated at the time of supply, not days or weeks later.

✅ Solution:

Integrate your POS with eTIMS or use the eTIMS mobile app for immediate invoicing.

❌

Mistake #8: Incorrect Time of Supply

Penalty: Interest on late payment

VAT is due at the earliest of: invoice date, payment date, or delivery date. Getting this wrong shifts VAT liability between periods.

✅ Solution:

Understand the time of supply rules. For continuous supplies, use consistent accounting.

❌

Mistake #9: Not Filing Nil Returns

Penalty: KES 10,000 per month

Even if you have no sales or purchases in a month, you must file a nil return.

✅ Solution:

File nil returns promptly. It takes 2 minutes and saves you KES 10,000 in penalties.

❌

Mistake #10: Poor Record Keeping

Penalty: KES 100,000

Failure to maintain proper VAT records for 5 years is an offense under the Tax Procedures Act.

✅ Solution:

Keep digital copies of all invoices, credit notes, and supporting documents. Organize by month and year.

Input VAT Recovery Rules

When Can You Claim Input VAT?

  • You have a valid tax invoice
  • The supplier is registered for VAT
  • The goods/services are for business purposes
  • The goods/services are used to make taxable supplies
  • You claim within 6 months of the invoice date

Restricted Input VAT (Partial Recovery)

Expense Type Recovery Rate
Entertainment (staff) 50%
Entertainment (clients) 0%
Passenger vehicles Limited to KES 3M cost
Club fees 0%
Medical expenses 0% (if for employees, different rules)

⚠️ Time Limit

Input VAT must be claimed within 6 months of the invoice date. After 6 months, you lose the right to claim.

Filing VAT Returns on iTax

1

Login to iTax

Access itax.kra.go.ke and login with your company credentials.

2

Select VAT Return

Go to "Returns" → "File Return" → Select "VAT Return (VAT 3)".

3

Enter Sales Information

Input total sales, zero-rated sales, exempt sales, and standard-rated sales.

4

Enter Purchases

Input purchases with input VAT, categorized by type (standard-rated, zero-rated).

5

Review Calculations

System calculates VAT payable or refundable. Verify all figures.

6

Submit and Pay

Submit return and generate payment slip if tax is due. Pay by the 20th.

eTIMS and VAT Compliance

eTIMS Integration

With eTIMS, VAT returns are partially auto-populated based on your issued invoices. This means:

  • Sales figures come directly from eTIMS invoices
  • Discrepancies trigger immediate alerts
  • Manual adjustments require explanations
  • Real-time visibility for KRA

💡 Best Practice

Run the eTIMS sales report and compare with your VAT return before filing. Reconcile any differences immediately.

VAT Audit Triggers

KRA may select your business for a VAT audit based on these red flags:

🔴 High-Risk Triggers

  • Consistent refund claims
  • Large fluctuations in sales
  • Negative VAT multiple months
  • eTIMS vs return mismatches

🟡 Medium-Risk Triggers

  • Late filings
  • Industry average variances
  • Related party transactions
  • New registration

VAT Refunds

If your input VAT exceeds output VAT for 3 consecutive months, you can apply for a refund.

Refund Process

  1. File VAT returns showing excess input VAT for 3 months
  2. Submit refund application via iTax
  3. KRA may conduct a pre-refund audit
  4. Refund processed within 90 days (theoretically)
  5. Interest accrues if refund delayed beyond 90 days

⚠️ Important

Refund applications often trigger audits. Ensure all your documentation is perfect before applying.

Special Rules for SMEs

Turnover Tax vs VAT

Small businesses can choose between:

  • Turnover Tax (3%): No VAT registration, no input claims
  • VAT Registration: Charge VAT, claim input, but more compliance

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the penalty for late VAT filing?

A: The greater of KES 10,000 or 5% of the tax due, plus 1% interest per month on unpaid tax.

Q: Can I claim VAT on petrol?

A: VAT on fuel is generally not claimable as it's considered a final consumer good, except for transporters.

Q: How long must I keep VAT records?

A: 5 years from the date of filing the return.

Q: What's the difference between zero-rated and exempt?

A: Zero-rated: 0% rate but can claim input VAT. Exempt: No VAT charged and no input VAT claimable.

Q: When must I register for VAT?

A: Within 30 days of exceeding KES 5 million turnover in any 12-month period.
